# Illinois Bitcoin Reserve and Bond Act (Draft)

> Draft working text for policy development. This document is illustrative and not legal advice.

## Synopsis

Creates the Illinois Bitcoin Reserve and Bond Act. Authorizes the State Treasurer, subject to appropriations and the requirements of this Act, to establish and maintain a strategic Bitcoin reserve. Provides for custody, audit, reporting, risk controls, and restrictions on unauthorized lending or rehypothecation. Authorizes the development of a bounded reserve-financing bond program, subject to all otherwise applicable constitutional, fiscal, debt-management, securities, and disclosure requirements of Illinois law. Provides for rulemaking, legislative reporting, and implementation. Effective immediately.

## Section 1. Short Title

This Act may be cited as the Illinois Bitcoin Reserve and Bond Act.

## Section 2. Legislative Findings

The General Assembly finds that:

1. Illinois should evaluate strategic treasury policies that may improve long-term fiscal resilience.
2. Bitcoin-related reserve or bond programs require explicit governance, disclosure, and oversight.
3. Any such program should be adopted only through clear statutory authority and reviewable public safeguards.

## Section 3. Definitions

In this Act:

- `"Bitcoin"` means the digital asset natively recorded on the Bitcoin blockchain.
- `"Strategic reserve"` means Bitcoin held by or for the State under this Act as a long-duration treasury asset subject to statutory safeguards.
- `"Qualified custodian"` means a custodian or control structure approved by the State Treasurer under standards adopted pursuant to this Act.
- `"Reserve-financing bond"` means an obligation issued or structured for the purpose of supporting one or more reserve-related purposes authorized by law, subject to all otherwise applicable constitutional and statutory limitations.
- `"Responsible State authority"` means the State Treasurer and any other State officer, agency, or financing body that must act under Illinois law for a transaction authorized by this Act.

## Section 4. Strategic Reserve Authorization

(a) Subject to appropriation and any other requirement of Illinois law, the State Treasurer may acquire, hold, safeguard, rebalance, and dispose of Bitcoin for the strategic reserve created by this Act.

(b) The State Treasurer may acquire Bitcoin only through procedures that:

1. identify the lawful source of funds;
2. document the acquisition authority;
3. maintain auditable records of acquisition price, timing, and custody; and
4. preserve public reporting consistent with this Act.

(c) The strategic reserve shall be operated as a rule-bound treasury program and not as a discretionary trading program.

(d) The State Treasurer shall establish and maintain:

1. allocation limits approved under State law;
2. custody and key-control standards;
3. restrictions on unauthorized lending, pledging, or rehypothecation; and
4. procedures governing any sale, transfer, or conversion of reserve holdings.

## Section 5. Bitcoin-Backed Bond Authority

(a) Subject to all otherwise applicable requirements of the Illinois Constitution, State debt law, budget law, securities law, disclosure obligations, and debt-management procedures, the responsible State authority may develop one or more reserve-financing bond proposals under this Act.

(b) A reserve-financing bond proposal under this Act must:

1. state its specific public purpose;
2. describe the proposed use of proceeds;
3. disclose the role of Bitcoin exposure in the proposed financing;
4. identify the manner of repayment or support;
5. describe the expected investor return profile and any bounded return-support features;
6. identify the principal risk factors, including volatility and legal-structure risk; and
7. identify any tranche, tier, or reserve-account features proposed for the issuance.

(c) A reserve-financing bond proposal may provide for the use of proceeds across one or more of the following purposes, to the extent otherwise permitted by law:

1. retirement, refinancing, or offset of outstanding State obligations;
2. acquisition of Bitcoin for the strategic reserve;
3. establishment of reserve accounts, debt-service support, or return-of-capital structures disclosed in the offering materials and authorized by law.

(d) Nothing in this Act shall be construed to waive, reduce, or supersede any constitutional limitation on State debt or any otherwise applicable requirement of Illinois law governing the issuance or support of obligations.

## Section 6. Transparency and Reporting

(a) The State Treasurer shall publish regular public reports concerning:

1. reserve holdings and custody controls;
2. material acquisitions, dispositions, or policy changes;
3. reserve-related expenses and service providers; and
4. any reserve-financing bond proposal advanced under this Act.

(b) Each report required under this Section shall include a plain-language summary suitable for legislative and public review.

(c) If a reserve-financing bond proposal is advanced under this Act, the responsible State authority shall provide a written disclosure summary explaining the proposed structure, principal risks, and the legal and fiscal reviews completed or still pending.

## Section 7. Rulemaking and Implementation

(a) The State Treasurer may adopt rules necessary to implement the reserve provisions of this Act.

(b) The State Treasurer shall consult with any other responsible State authority whose approval or participation is required by Illinois law for a reserve-financing bond proposal.

(c) Implementation under this Act shall remain subject to appropriation, applicable debt and disclosure law, and any additional review required by the General Assembly or other authority under Illinois law.

## Section 8. Construction

Nothing in this Act shall be interpreted as requiring the State to issue a bond, acquire Bitcoin in excess of lawfully authorized limits, or bypass any constitutional, statutory, fiscal, or disclosure requirement otherwise applicable to State financial operations.

## Section 99. Effective Date

This Act takes effect upon becoming law.

## Drafting Notes

- Final bill text should be reviewed by Illinois legislative drafting staff and qualified counsel.
- Bond language should be narrowed further only after state-specific constitutional, debt-structure, securities, and fiscal analysis.
- This draft intentionally presents one primary reserve-financing Bitcoin-backed bond model while leaving tranche design, percentage splits, and exact yield mechanics open for later legal and finance review.
