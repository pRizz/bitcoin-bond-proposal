# Florida HB487

## At a Glance

Florida HB487 was one of the more consequential 2025 reserve-side state bills because it applied Bitcoin investment authority across multiple public-fund buckets in a large state system. It also went beyond simple reserve language by addressing lending, payment handling, and retirement-system investing.

## What This Would Actually Do

HB487 would have authorized the Chief Financial Officer to invest money from the General Revenue Fund, Budget Stabilization Fund, agency and judicial trust funds, and other designated state funds in Bitcoin, subject to a 10 percent cap for any account. It also would have allowed the trustees of the State Board of Administration to invest available System Trust Fund money in Bitcoin and permitted state-held Bitcoin lending if the loans did not increase financial risk.

## Bond / Reserve Analysis

**Supports:** direct reserve accumulation  
**Bond-financed reserve accumulation:** no meaningful support  
**Meaningful reading:** reserve-heavy, but broader than a pure reserve custody bill

Florida matters because the bill scaled the reserve concept across meaningful public-fund pools instead of limiting it to a symbolic pilot. It is limited for the bond thesis because it did not create a financing mechanism and never moved beyond one favorable subcommittee.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Authorizes CFO Bitcoin investment from multiple named state funds.
- Caps Bitcoin investment at 10 percent of the total funds in any account.
- Allows custody directly, through a qualified custodian, or through an exchange-traded product.
- Allows Bitcoin lending under CFO rulemaking if the loans do not increase financial risk.
- Directs taxes and fees paid in Bitcoin into General Revenue with reimbursement of designated funds in U.S. dollars.
- Allows SBA trustees to invest available System Trust Fund money in Bitcoin.

## Why This Matters

Florida is a useful reserve-side benchmark because it shows what a large-state public-funds Bitcoin bill can look like when it is drafted seriously enough to move through committee. It is also a cautionary record because even a broader, more operationally detailed bill can still stall well before enactment.
