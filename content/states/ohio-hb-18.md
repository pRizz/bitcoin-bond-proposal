# Ohio HB 18

## At a Glance

Ohio HB 18 is an active early-stage House proposal. It would create a state treasurer digital-asset interim-fund authority and also amend state retirement-system investment rules for exchange-traded products, so it should be read as a broader strategic cryptocurrency reserve proposal rather than a bitcoin-only reserve bill.

## What This Would Actually Do

HB 18 would permit the Treasurer of State to invest certain interim funds in digital assets and would amend state retirement-system statutes to address investments in exchange-traded products. The introduced text defines digital assets broadly, requires digital assets to be an exchange-traded product for the interim-moneys authority, and caps those investments at ten percent of the balance.

## Bond / Reserve Analysis

**Supports:** direct digital-asset reserve authority for interim public funds  
**Bond-financed reserve accumulation:** no meaningful support in the introduced bill  
**Meaningful reading:** active mixed digital-asset reserve proposal

Ohio matters because HB 18 is a current official state bill that would give the Treasurer of State explicit authority to invest interim public moneys in qualifying digital assets. It is still in committee, so the record is useful as a live legislative signal rather than an enacted policy model.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Authorizes the Treasurer of State to invest interim moneys in qualifying digital assets.
- Defines digital assets as virtual currency, cryptocurrencies, natively electronic assets, stablecoins, nonfungible tokens, and other digital-only assets with economic, proprietary, or access rights.
- Requires the relevant digital assets to be an exchange-traded product.
- Caps aggregate interim-moneys digital-asset investments at ten percent of the balance.
- Amends retirement-system investment provisions for exchange-traded products.

## Why This Matters

HB 18 shows Ohio considering public-funds digital-asset exposure through existing treasury and retirement-system investment channels. The bill is important to track, but its committee-stage posture and broader digital-asset scope mean it should not be presented as enacted law or as a narrow bitcoin reserve.
