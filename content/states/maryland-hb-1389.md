## What This Would Actually Do

HB1389 would create the Maryland Bitcoin Reserve Fund and let the State Treasurer use that fund to hold bitcoin as a reserve asset for the State. The bill routes certain money seized and forfeited to the State under gambling-violation provisions into the fund, allows the fund to receive appropriations and other money, and limits fund use to bitcoin investment.

## Bond / Reserve Analysis

**Supports:** direct reserve accumulation  
**Bond-financed reserve accumulation:** no meaningful support  
**Meaningful reading:** reserve-only

Maryland matters because it offers an official reserve proposal with a specific funding pathway rather than only a general authorization to study or hold bitcoin. It does not create a bond-financing structure and should not be read as a bond-side model.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Establishes the Maryland Bitcoin Reserve Fund as a special, nonlapsing fund.
- Directs certain seized and forfeited gambling money into the fund.
- Allows the fund to receive appropriations, interest earnings, and other accepted money.
- Limits the fund's use to investment in bitcoin.
- Requires bitcoin to be held either by the State Treasurer through a secure institutional platform or by a qualified custodian.

## Why This Matters

This is a credible publication candidate because the Maryland General Assembly record provides both the bill text and a tracked hearing status. It also broadens the registry with a reserve model built around a distinct source of state-controlled funds rather than a general treasury-allocation cap.
