## What This Would Actually Do

H4256 would create a Strategic Digital Assets Reserve and authorize the State Treasurer to invest unexpended and uncommitted money from the General Fund, the Budget Stabilization Reserve Fund, and other Treasurer-managed investment funds into digital assets including bitcoin. The bill layers in allocation caps, custody options, reporting obligations, public-address disclosure, donation procedures, and security-audit expectations.

South Carolina matters because it shows a legislature filing a fairly developed reserve framework with portfolio rules, custody language, and reporting mechanics. It does not create a bond-financing path and should remain in the reserve lane for editorial purposes.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Authorizes investment from the General Fund, Budget Stabilization Reserve Fund, and other Treasurer-managed funds.
- Caps investment from an eligible fund at ten percent of funds under management.
- Requires holdings to remain in secure custody, with qualified-custodian and exchange-traded-product options.
- Adds a separate limit on the amount of each digital asset within the State's digital-asset portfolio.
- Requires biennial reporting, publication of public reserve addresses, and a resident donation process.
