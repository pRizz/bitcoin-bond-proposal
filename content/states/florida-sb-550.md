# Florida SB 550

## At a Glance

Florida SB 550 is a failed public-funds Bitcoin investment proposal. It is useful as an official-source record of legislative interest in state-managed Bitcoin exposure, but it should be read as an inactive 2025 bill rather than a live reserve program.

## What This Would Actually Do

SB 550 would have authorized the Chief Financial Officer to invest money in Bitcoin from certain public funds. The filed bill text also would have authorized the trustees of the State Board of Administration to invest and reinvest available System Trust Fund assets in Bitcoin.

## Bond / Reserve Analysis

**Supports:** direct reserve or reserve-adjacent public-fund Bitcoin investment authority  
**Bond-financed reserve accumulation:** no meaningful support  
**Meaningful reading:** reserve-side public-funds signal

Florida matters because the official bill text addresses public-fund Bitcoin investment authority directly. It is limited for the bond thesis because it does not create a public-debt financing mechanism and because the proposal died in committee.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Would authorize the Chief Financial Officer to invest certain public-fund money in Bitcoin.
- Would authorize State Board of Administration trustees to invest and reinvest available System Trust Fund assets in Bitcoin.
- Uses Bitcoin-specific public-fund investment language rather than a bond-financed accumulation structure.
- Did not become law after SB 550 Died in Senate Banking and Insurance and companion HB 487 Died in the House Government Operations Subcommittee.

## Why This Is Limited

SB 550 is strong evidence that public-fund Bitcoin investment authority reached the Florida Legislature in official bill form. It is also a terminal failed record, so it should not be treated as enacted authority or current legislative momentum.
