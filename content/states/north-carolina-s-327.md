# North Carolina S327

## At a Glance

North Carolina S327 is the clearest official crossover record in this batch because the filed bill combines a Bitcoin reserve proposal with explicit language allowing the reserve to back bonds for public projects. It is still only an introduced Senate bill, so the bond-side signal is conceptual rather than operational.

## What This Would Actually Do

S327 would authorize the Office of the State Treasurer to allocate up to ten percent of public funds into bitcoin and hold those assets in a State-managed reserve. The bill also authorizes yield-generating strategies, creates a Bitcoin Economic Advisory Board, requires custody and audit measures, and expressly says the reserve may be used to back bonds as an alternative financing mechanism for public projects.

## Bond / Reserve Analysis

**Supports:** direct reserve accumulation and reserve-backed bond framing  
**Bond-financed reserve accumulation:** explicit conceptual support, but no mature issuance structure  
**Meaningful reading:** reserve-plus-bond crossover signal

North Carolina matters because it is one of the few official state records in this project that directly joins reserve language with bond language in the same filed text. It remains early and unusually broad, so it should be read as an official signal of combined intent rather than as a ready-to-implement public-finance template.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Authorizes allocation of up to ten percent of public funds into bitcoin.
- Requires cold-storage wallets with multi-signature authentication and a State-controlled custody department.
- Creates a Bitcoin Economic Advisory Board.
- Requires monthly audits and State-backed insurance policies.
- Allows the reserve to be used to back bonds as an alternative financing mechanism for public projects.

## Why This Matters

This entry broadens the registry in a substantively different direction from the other Phase 11 states. Most of the batch is reserve-only or reserve-adjacent. North Carolina is useful because the official filed text itself raises the reserve-plus-bond idea that sits closer to the project's broader thesis.
