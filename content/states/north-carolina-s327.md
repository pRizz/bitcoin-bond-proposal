# North Carolina S327

## At a Glance

North Carolina S327 is the clearest Phase 11 bridge between the registry’s reserve coverage and the project’s bond-financed public-finance thesis. It is still primarily a reserve bill, but unlike most state records in this space, it says directly that the reserve may be used to back bonds for public projects.

## What This Would Actually Do

S327 would authorize the Office of the State Treasurer to allocate up to 10 percent of public funds into Bitcoin and hold those assets in a formal Bitcoin Reserve. The bill also would authorize Bitcoin-backed investment strategies such as lending, require cold-storage and multi-signature custody, mandate monthly audits, encourage mining exploration, and permit reserve use for critical projects, research incentives, and bond backing.

## Bond / Reserve Analysis

**Supports:** direct reserve accumulation and reserve-backed financing  
**Bond-financed reserve accumulation:** meaningful support  
**Meaningful reading:** both, with reserve-first architecture and explicit financing language

North Carolina matters because it is one of the few official state bills that makes the financing bridge explicit instead of leaving it as an inference. It is still limited because it never advanced beyond referral to committee and because several sections read more like an ambitious concept bill than a narrowed treasury implementation package.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Authorizes up to 10 percent of public funds to be allocated into Bitcoin.
- Creates a formal Bitcoin Reserve under the Office of the State Treasurer.
- Allows lending and other regulated yield-generating Bitcoin-backed strategies.
- Requires cold storage, multi-signature controls, monthly audits, and state-backed insurance policies.
- Directs the Treasurer to explore mining as a way to increase state holdings.
- Limits reserve use to specific purposes, including crisis response, infrastructure financing, research incentives, and backing bonds for public projects.

## Why This Matters

For this project’s thesis, North Carolina is unusually important because it makes reserve-backed bond use an express statutory feature. That does not make it a finished bond-financing blueprint, but it does make it one of the cleanest public-record signals that state bitcoin reserve policy can be drafted with financing applications in mind.
