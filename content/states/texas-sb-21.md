## What This Would Actually Do

SB21 creates the Texas Strategic Bitcoin Reserve and Investment Act and establishes a reserve outside the state treasury that is administered by the comptroller. The enacted text authorizes the reserve to hold bitcoin and other qualifying cryptocurrency, sets custody and audit mechanics, authorizes an advisory committee, and requires biennial reporting.

## Bond / Reserve Analysis

**Supports:** direct reserve accumulation  
**Bond-financed reserve accumulation:** no meaningful support in the enacted law  
**Meaningful reading:** reserve-heavy benchmark

SB21 matters because it creates a serious state reserve framework with operative custody, governance, and reporting rules. It is limited for the bond thesis because the enacted law does not create a reserve-financing bond or comparable public-debt accumulation mechanism.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Creates the Texas Strategic Bitcoin Reserve and Investment Act.
- Places the reserve outside the state treasury and under comptroller administration.
- Allows reserve holdings in bitcoin and other qualifying cryptocurrency with a market-cap threshold.
- Permits use of qualified custodians, qualified liquidity providers, audits, and an advisory committee.
- Requires biennial reporting to the legislature and publication on the comptroller’s website.

## Why This Matters

Texas is the strongest current reserve-law benchmark in the first registry batch. It shows what a serious enacted reserve structure can look like, even though it does not yet supply the bond-financed accumulation path this project prefers.
