# Ohio SB57

## At a Glance

Ohio SB57 is a serious reserve-side state record because it creates a named bitcoin reserve fund inside the treasury and imposes explicit holding, custody, and reporting rules. It is also broader than many reserve bills because it links the reserve concept to state acceptance of cryptocurrency payments.

## What This Would Actually Do

SB57 would create the Ohio bitcoin reserve fund in the state treasury and allow the Treasurer of State to use interim state money and reserve-fund deposits to acquire Bitcoin as an investment. The bill would require a five-year holding period, allow secure custody either directly or through a qualified custodian, permit donations into the fund, and require state entities to accept cryptocurrency payments through a treasury-managed conversion and settlement process.

It is limited for the bond thesis because it does not connect the reserve to debt issuance or public-project financing, and it remained in committee.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Creates the Ohio bitcoin reserve fund in the state treasury.
- Allows the Treasurer of State to use interim state money and fund deposits to acquire Bitcoin.
- Requires a minimum five-year holding period before later sale, transfer, or conversion.
- Requires secure custody through the Treasurer or a qualified custodian.
- Allows gifts, grants, and donations of Bitcoin to the reserve fund.
- Requires state entities to accept cryptocurrency payments with treasury-managed handling and reporting rules.

## Why This Matters

Ohio is a strong reserve benchmark because it combines a named reserve fund with detailed treasury operations and reporting language. That makes it more concrete than many symbolic reserve proposals, even though it does not meaningfully advance the financing side of the project’s thesis.
