# Arizona SB1025

## At a Glance

Arizona SB1025 is a major reserve-side signal because it would have allowed public funds to allocate a defined portion of assets into virtual currency holdings. It is also a useful cautionary record because it did not become law.

## What This Would Actually Do

SB1025 would have allowed major Arizona public funds to invest up to 10 percent of public monies under their control in virtual currency holdings. In practice, it would have expanded digital-asset investment authority for the Treasurer and two large retirement systems rather than creating a narrowly ring-fenced bitcoin-only reserve program.

## Bond / Reserve Analysis

**Supports:** direct reserve or reserve-adjacent accumulation  
**Bond-financed reserve accumulation:** no meaningful support  
**Meaningful reading:** reserve-heavy and broader than bitcoin-only branding suggests

Arizona matters because it is a high-visibility reserve-side proposal. It is limited for the bond thesis because it does not create a financing mechanism and because its operative scope is broader than a pure bitcoin treasury reserve.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Allows up to 10 percent investment of controlled public monies in virtual currency holdings.
- Applies to the State Treasurer, Arizona State Retirement System, and Public Safety Personnel Retirement System.
- Uses a broader virtual-currency concept instead of a narrow bitcoin-only statutory structure.
- Was ultimately vetoed, so none of the proposed investment authority took effect.

## Why This Is Limited

SB1025 is important as a reserve-side signal, but it is not a bond-financed reserve model and it is not enacted law. It should therefore be read as evidence of legislative direction, not as an operational state reserve framework.
