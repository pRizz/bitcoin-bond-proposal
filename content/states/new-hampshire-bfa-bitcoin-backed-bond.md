## What This Would Actually Do

The BFA board approved a resolution authorizing up to $100 million in bonds over time for a project to acquire and hold digital currency, subject to receipt of counsel’s opinion that the bond is duly authorized and permitted. The BFA later announced the structure publicly as a bitcoin-backed municipal bond and said issuance would occur upon approval by the Governor and Executive Council.

## Key Structural Mechanics

- Board approved a resolution under RSA 162-I authorizing up to $100 million in bonds for a project to acquire and hold digital currency.
- Approval was conditioned on receiving an opinion of counsel that the bond is duly authorized and permitted under New Hampshire law.
- The public announcement describes the issuance as a bitcoin-backed municipal bond and identifies institutional participants including Wave Digital Assets, Rosemawr Management, BitGo Trust, and Orrick.
- The public announcement explicitly says no taxpayer funds or state guarantees are at risk.

## Why This Matters

This is the clearest official bond-side exemplar for the registry because it directly supports the strategic idea of bond-financed digital-asset accumulation. It is also limited because it is not a legislature-filed state reserve bill and should not be described as if it were one.
