## What This Would Actually Do

HB4087 would allow the State Treasurer to invest money from the general fund and the countercyclical budget and economic stabilization fund in cryptocurrency, subject to a ten-percent cap for each fund. The bill also addresses treatment of taxes or fees received in cryptocurrency, custody methods, and potential lending of state-held cryptocurrency if additional risk is not created.

Michigan matters because it is a serious public-finance authorization bill rather than a symbolic recognition measure. It remains limited for the bond thesis because it does not pair reserve accumulation with public-debt issuance or a bond-backed reserve structure.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Authorizes investment from the general fund and the budget stabilization fund.
- Caps cryptocurrency investment at ten percent of available money in each eligible fund.
- Requires custody through a secure custody solution, a qualified custodian, or an exchange-traded product.
- Directs cryptocurrency tax or fee receipts into the general fund with reimbursement mechanics for designated funds.
- Permits lending of held cryptocurrency only through rulemaking and only if the State's financial risk is not increased.
