## What This Would Actually Do

HB 1184 would have authorized the Board of University and School Lands, the State Investment Board, and the State Treasurer to invest up to ten percent of specified funds in precious metals, qualifying digital assets, or stablecoins. The official text uses a market capitalization threshold of more than $500 billion for digital assets.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Would authorize the Board of University and School Lands to invest up to ten percent of specified funds.
- Would authorize the State Investment Board to invest up to ten percent of specified funds.
- Would authorize the State Treasurer to invest up to ten percent of specified funds.
- Eligible assets include precious metals, digital assets with market capitalization exceeding $500 billion, or stablecoins.
- Failed on House second reading with 32 yeas and 57 nays.

## Why This Is Limited

HB 1184 is credible official evidence of North Dakota considering digital-asset investment authority, but it failed on the House floor. The bill also sits outside a narrow bitcoin-only reserve model because it includes precious metals, qualifying digital assets, and stablecoins.
