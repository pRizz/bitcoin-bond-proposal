## At a Glance

Wyoming HB0201 is a failed state-funds Bitcoin investment proposal. It is useful as an official-source record of legislative interest in capped public Bitcoin exposure, but it should be read as an inactive 2025 bill rather than enacted authority or current momentum.

## What This Would Actually Do

HB0201 would have authorized the State Treasurer to invest specified state funds and permanent funds in Bitcoin. The official introduced text set a three-percent cap for the general fund and separate three-percent caps for the permanent Wyoming mineral trust fund and permanent land fund, with custody through a secure custody solution, qualified custodian, or exchange-traded product.

## Bond / Reserve Analysis

**Supports:** capped state-fund and permanent-fund Bitcoin investment authority  
**Bond-financed reserve accumulation:** no meaningful support  
**Meaningful reading:** reserve-side public-funds signal

Wyoming matters because HB0201 used Bitcoin-specific state-funds language and included custody and reporting mechanics. It is limited for the bond thesis because it did not create a public-debt financing mechanism and because the bill died in committee.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Would authorize Bitcoin investment for the general fund, permanent Wyoming mineral trust fund, and permanent land fund.
- Would cap general-fund Bitcoin exposure at three percent.
- Would cap permanent-fund Bitcoin exposure at three percent for each covered fund.
- Would require custody through a secure custody solution, qualified custodian, or exchange-traded product.
- Received a failed H09 Minerals do-pass vote of 1-7-1-0-0 on February 10, 2025.
- Died in committee and was returned pursuant to House Rule 5-4 on March 3, 2025.

## Why This Is Limited

HB0201 is credible official evidence that Wyoming considered capped Bitcoin investment authority for state and permanent funds, but it became inactive after dying in committee. This entry should not be read as enacted authority or as a bond-financed reserve structure.
