# Wyoming HB0201

## At a Glance

Wyoming HB0201 is a clean reserve-side state-funds bill rather than a symbolic resolution or branding exercise. Its importance is that it explicitly reaches both ordinary state funds and long-horizon permanent funds, even though the bill died early in committee.

## What This Would Actually Do

HB0201 would authorize the State Treasurer to invest the general fund, Permanent Wyoming Mineral Trust Fund, and Permanent Land Fund in Bitcoin, subject to a 3 percent cap for each covered fund. It would allow direct secure custody, qualified custodians, or approved exchange-traded products, and it would explicitly allow appreciated holdings above the cap to remain in place without forced selling.

Wyoming matters because it uses straightforward public-funds allocation language and reaches permanent funds that legislatures often treat more cautiously than ordinary cash accounts. It is limited because it died quickly in committee and never developed a broader financing or treasury-operations framework around the reserve concept.

## Key Statutory Mechanics

- Authorizes Bitcoin investment from the general fund, Permanent Wyoming Mineral Trust Fund, and Permanent Land Fund.
- Caps Bitcoin exposure at 3 percent of each covered fund.
- Allows direct secure custody, a qualified custodian, or an exchange-traded product.
- Does not force sales when price appreciation alone pushes holdings above the cap.
- Requires the State Treasurer to promulgate rules to implement the investment program.

## Why This Matters

Wyoming is a useful benchmark because it shows how a reserve bill can be written to touch both general-fund and permanent-fund capital explicitly. That makes it a stronger public-record signal than a purely rhetorical reserve proposal, even though the legislature rejected it early.
