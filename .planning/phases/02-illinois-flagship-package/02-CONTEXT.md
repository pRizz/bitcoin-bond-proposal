## Implementation Decisions

### Reserve policy assumptions
- The Illinois reserve concept should authorize the state to acquire, hold, custody, rebalance, and report on Bitcoin within a tightly defined strategic reserve program.
- The packet should not frame the reserve as broad discretionary trading authority.
- Reserve funding should be framed conservatively through specifically authorized appropriations, transfers, or programmatic allocations approved by law.
- The Illinois packet should make these reserve guardrails explicit:
  - statutory allocation cap
  - custody requirements
  - public reporting cadence
  - auditability
  - prohibition on unauthorized lending or rehypothecation
  - defined approval authority for purchases and dispositions
- The reserve should be presented as phased and bounded, not maximalist.

### Bond policy assumptions
- The Illinois packet should use a deliberately conservative umbrella definition of “Bitcoin-backed bond”.
- The main Illinois model should be closest to a reserve-financing bond with explicit disclosure, rather than a highly experimental collateralized structure.
- The packet should frame the concept as a public-finance analogue to Bitcoin accumulation financing, not as a direct copy of any private-company instrument.
- The state should be described as taking the long-duration Bitcoin volatility exposure because it is the long-term holder.
- Bondholders should be described as receiving a bounded return profile tied to the program, with yield meaningfully above plain Treasuries but below aggressive private Bitcoin-linked instruments.
- The bond discussion may mention tranche or tier variants as options, but the Illinois packet should present one primary structure first and treat alternatives as bounded variants rather than the default.
- The packet may describe a conservative conceptual split where proceeds could be allocated across:
  - retirement or offset of current outstanding government debt
  - Bitcoin accumulation
  - return-of-capital or structured return support for bond buyers
- Exact percentages and yield math remain open and should not be prematurely locked in Phase 2.
- Every bond discussion should carry these caveats:
  - illustrative model only
  - requires state-specific constitutional review
  - requires securities and disclosure review
  - requires fiscal and debt-management analysis
  - official legal drafting and finance review control over the final structure
- The overall tone should be serious and innovative, but fiscally disciplined rather than speculative.

### One-pager shape and tone
- The one-pager headline should feel serious and policy-forward, not slogan-heavy.
- The preferred style is closer to:
  - “A Disciplined Illinois Bitcoin Reserve and Bond Framework”
- The one-pager should remain truly one page and use roughly 5-6 tight blocks:
  - thesis
  - why Illinois
  - reserve model
  - bond model
  - safeguards
  - legislative ask or next step
- The persuasion balance should lead with fiscal discipline, competitiveness, and debt-management logic first.
- Bitcoin upside should appear as the strategic rationale, but not as the opening emotional pitch.
- The one-pager should include a compact bounded-risk or legal-review-required section, but it should remain brief enough that the document still reads like an argument for action rather than a caution memo.

### Draft bill and methodology depth
- The Phase 2 draft bill should read like a serious counsel-ready first draft with:
  - real sections
  - operative language
  - definitions
  - authorization
  - guardrails
  - reporting
  - implementation provisions
- The bill should still carry explicit drafting caveats where appropriate.
- The bill should stay intentionally open on:
  - exact bond mechanics
  - percentage splits
  - tranche structure
  - implementation details that require legal and fiscal review
- The methodology memo should remain the governing trust and classification contract for the whole project.
- Phase 2 may add Illinois-specific explanatory passages only where they directly justify how the packet uses those rules.
- The packet should present one main Illinois bond model in the one-pager and bill narrative.
- The packet may note 2-3 bounded variants in methodology or drafting notes so the package stays decisive without pretending there is only one conceivable structure.

- Exact placeholder structure for open bond mechanics, as long as the packet stays serious and not hand-wavy.
