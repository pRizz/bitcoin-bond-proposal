# Phase 2: Illinois Flagship Package - Research

**Researched:** 2026-04-01
**Status:** Ready for planning

## Objective

Research what Phase 2 needs so the Illinois packet can become a serious first public and legislative-facing deliverable rather than a placeholder document set.

## Key Illinois-Specific Inputs

### 1. Illinois already has a live reserve-bill reference point

The official Illinois General Assembly record for [HB1844](https://www.ilga.gov/Legislation/BillStatus?DocNum=1844&DocTypeID=HB&GA=104&GAID=18&LegId=159334&SessionID=114) gives the project a real state-specific baseline.

What matters for planning:

- it is titled `STRATEGIC BITCOIN RESERVE`;
- it was introduced on January 29, 2025 and referred to the House Rules Committee on January 29, 2025;
- it is reserve-only, not a reserve-plus-bond package;
- it uses a real Illinois act structure with:
  - short title
  - findings and purpose
  - definitions
  - reserve-fund creation
  - management/security provisions
  - reporting
  - donations and conversion provisions
  - rulemaking

Implication:

- Phase 2 should not invent Illinois packet structure from scratch.
- The draft bill should borrow the seriousness and sectioning conventions of the official Illinois reserve bill while deliberately extending into a more bounded reserve-plus-bond model.

### 2. Illinois bill format conventions are documented by the Legislative Reference Bureau

The official [Illinois Bill Drafting Manual](https://www.ilga.gov/commission/lrb/Manual.pdf) and [Legislative Reference Bureau site](https://www.ilga.gov/commission/lrb_home.html) matter because the packet is supposed to read like a serious first-pass legislative draft.

Planning-relevant takeaways:

- the synopsis is a statutory requirement and is meant to summarize the substance of the bill at a glance;
- `New Act` is the standard synopsis indicator when a bill creates a new Act;
- the manual shows examples of synopsis structure, effective-date handling, and LRB draft naming conventions;
- the LRB is the official drafting body for most Illinois legislation, so the packet should read as something a legislative drafter could work with, not as marketing copy in bill form.

Implication:

- The Phase 2 bill plan should include synopsis-quality front matter or drafting support content, even if the canonical markdown file is not pretending to be an official introduced bill PDF.
- The final draft bill should preserve real bill-style sectioning and effective-date logic.

### 3. Illinois constitutional debt language creates a real planning boundary

The official Illinois Constitution, [Article IX, Section 9](https://www.ilga.gov/commission/lrb/con9.htm), is the most important legal boundary for the bond side of the packet.

Planning-relevant takeaways:

- state debt is tightly defined when it is secured by the State’s full faith and credit or repaid directly or indirectly from tax revenue;
- debt for specific purposes requires either a three-fifths vote in each house or approval by voters, depending on the path used;
- any law incurring or guaranteeing debt must state the specific purposes and the manner of repayment;
- subsection (f) separately contemplates obligations not secured by full faith and credit or tax revenue, if authorized by law.

Implication:

- The packet should not overstate the legal settledness of any Illinois bond model.
- The bill should be serious enough to describe a primary bond concept, but still preserve explicit legal and fiscal review caveats.
- The methodology and bill drafting notes should acknowledge that the bond structure may need to be refined depending on whether the eventual model is framed as state debt, non-full-faith-and-credit indebtedness, or another legally authorized financing mechanism.

## Planning Implications

### One-pager

The one-pager should move from a structural placeholder to a true packet front page:

- headline first, slogan-light;
- lead with fiscal discipline and debt-management logic;
- summarize one primary reserve model and one primary bond model;
- keep caveats visible but compact;
- fit the actual print constraints of the PDF lane already in Phase 1.

### Draft bill

The draft bill should become a counsel-ready first-pass document:

- retain official-style sectioning;
- use operational language where the packet has enough confidence;
- leave clearly marked openings where bond percentages, tranche options, or specific financing mechanics still need legal and fiscal review;
- use the existing HB1844 structure as a baseline reference for reserve sections and reporting/security style.

### Methodology memo

The methodology memo should remain the trust contract, but Phase 2 should make it clearly usable as part of the Illinois packet:

- explain why the Illinois packet is presenting one primary bond model with bounded variants;
- clarify that state-specific constitutional and securities review still governs the final bond structure;
- keep the public research methodology stable while adding packet-facing context where needed.

## Recommended Plan Split

The cleanest split is three plans in two waves:

### Wave 1

- **02-01 Illinois one-pager**
  - turn the one-pager into a serious legislator-facing summary;
  - update its PDF output.

- **02-02 Illinois draft bill**
  - turn the draft into a serious first-pass reserve-plus-bond act;
  - update its PDF output.

These plans can run in parallel because they mainly touch different primary artifacts.

### Wave 2

- **02-03 Methodology and packet alignment**
  - update methodology for Illinois packet use;
  - align terminology and caveats across the packet;
  - regenerate and verify the packet PDFs together.

## Risks and Gotchas

- The bill can easily become either too vague or too overconfident; Phase 2 should land in the middle.
- The bond concept is the riskiest part of the packet because it can drift into speculative framing if not kept bounded.
- The one-pager must remain actually one page in the current PDF lane or it will stop serving its intended audience.
- If terminology drifts between the one-pager, bill, and methodology memo, the packet will look unserious.
