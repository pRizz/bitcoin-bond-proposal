<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### First explainer topic
- The first deep explainer should focus on the financing logic rather than basic reserve definitions.
- The preferred topic is:
  - why bond-financed reserve accumulation is different from simply buying Bitcoin with taxpayer funds
- The explainer should be strongly argued but still source-first and policy-literate.
- The explainer should use this argumentative structure:
  - claim
  - mechanism
  - objections
  - limits
  - why the preferred model still matters
- The explainer should focus on the contrast between reserve-only logic and bond-financed reserve accumulation.
- The desired takeaway is:
  - bond-financed reserve accumulation is the more politically and fiscally serious path
  - reserve-only proposals matter but are incomplete
  - the distinction is structural, not branding

### Editorial polish targets
- The highest-priority polish target is the homepage hero and the first two section transitions.
- The homepage copy should be refined by:
  - tightening the hero subcopy
  - sharpening the proof-strip labels
  - making the Illinois packet feature feel more inevitable and less optional
- Layout and pacing polish should focus on:
  - stronger spacing contrast between hero and proof sections
  - more deliberate asymmetry in one or two key sections
  - cleaner handoff from thesis to registry proof
- Even in polish, the site should stay restrained:
  - no new major sections
  - no extra controls
  - no stats clutter
  - no decorative motion for its own sake

### Infographic refinement
- The first visual to upgrade should be the reserve-versus-bond pathway diagram.
- That visual should help readers understand:
  - where capital comes from
  - who holds the volatility risk
  - who receives the bounded return
  - why this differs from taxpayer-funded spot accumulation
- The second upgraded visual should be the registry proof or state-status view.
- It should remain more of a grouped proof visual than a dense map in this phase.
- Motion should be noticeable but restrained, using:
  - progressive reveals
  - subtle line or node emphasis
  - soft hover or state transitions
- Motion should not turn the site into an animated infographic product.

### Public voice and edge
- The editorial voice should become sharper than in Phase 4, but remain disciplined.
- The site should sound like a serious editorial research product that is comfortable making judgments.
- Dry edge or restrained satire should stay low but noticeable.
- That edge should appear mostly in:
  - explainer subheads
  - why this matters / why this is limited lines
  - occasional homepage or support copy
- The following surfaces should remain plain and neutral:
  - legislative status blocks
  - source trails
  - methodology rules
  - record-type labels

### Claude's Discretion
- Exact explainer title and section naming, so long as it stays centered on the approved financing contrast.
- Exact hero-copy refinements and section-transition pacing choices.
- Exact visual treatment of the upgraded pathway and registry-proof graphics, as long as they remain explanatory first.
- Exact placement and phrasing of dry editorial edge in non-factual surfaces.
- Exact animation and interaction details, so long as motion remains restrained.

</decisions>
